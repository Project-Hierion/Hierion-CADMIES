Fractals

Geometric or mathematical structures that exhibit self-similarity across scales — zoom in on any portion and you find patterns that echo the whole. Fractals are produced by iterative processes: simple rules applied repeatedly generate infinite complexity. They are found throughout nature (coastlines, ferns, blood vessels, snowflakes, galaxies) and mathematics (Mandelbrot set, Koch snowflake, Sierpinski triangle). Fractals reveal that complexity does not require complex rules — it requires iteration. They are the visual and mathematical expression of the principle that micro-level recursion produces macro-level richness. 'Only scale is the difference' is a fractal truth.
