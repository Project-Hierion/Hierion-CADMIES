What we can actually pull from:

Shattuck's Forbidden Knowledge — the bibliography alone is a roadmap. Prometheus to pornography. What got suppressed, by whom, and why. This is the spine for dataset #1.

The Sphaera Project — 350 textbook editions, 1472–1650, knowledge graph with metadata. How astronomical knowledge moved across Europe. Perfect for dataset #2, the transmission story.

EMDFL Dataset — 55,000 early modern dissertations, 1564–1800. What academics were actually writing about. The granular detail of knowledge circulation.

Winchester's Knowing What We Know — broad history of knowledge transmission, bibliography gives us the canon.

Digital Papyrology tools — Trismegistos, papyri.info. Ancient written knowledge. Where the story starts.

For dataset #3 (Bibliography & Translation), the Sphaera knowledge graph is especially interesting — it's built on CIDOC-CRM, an ontology for cultural heritage data. That's exactly the kind of structured metadata a librarian would understand instinctively. Teaching Dr. Mistral about ontologies, citation chains, and translation drift through real examples.
