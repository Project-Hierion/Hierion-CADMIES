## MONTH-BY-MONTH BREAKDOWN

### January — The Lottery

- Could be 75°. Could be 32°. Could be both. In the same day.
    
- Dress warm unless it's warm outside.
    
- Someone in a winter coat and someone in sandals and sweatpants at the same HEB. Both are correct.
    

### February — Actually Kinda Cold

- This is when South Texas remembers it's technically on Earth.
    
- Might hit 32° for a couple hours before sunrise. Might hit 36° with a high of 50°.
    
- No ice storms. No blizzards. That's north Texas. That's DFW. That's not us.
    
- Still, 36° feels like an ice storm when you're used to 96°.
    
- Bundle up, complain, wait for March.
    

### March — Hoodie Roulette

- Morning: hoodie. Afternoon: 80s. Overnight: possibly winter again.
    
- Rain is a beer pong game — maybe it comes, maybe it doesn't.
    
- Spring showers exist in theory. It either doesn't rain or it pours down.
    

### April — The Pretty Week

- One good week, maybe. Sundresses. Cowboy boots. Hats. Everyone looks good and feels good and we all know it.
    
- Men: hoodie season's over. Match the energy.
    
- The rest of April is March with higher expectations. Sometimes just lots of rain.
    

### May — False Hope

- You were hoping it would be cool. Mid-80s to low 90s. Humidity 85-90%.
    
- Thunderstorms 4.5 days a week.
    
- Occasional **cool front** — not a cold front. Just the freezer door opening briefly. Nice. Still in shorts. Sleeves still rolled. Don't get used to it.
    
- This is just the warmup. August is loading.
    

### June — The Ramp Up

- 90s become standard. Humidity locks in.
    
- The sun transitions from "bright" to "personal vendetta."
    
- Cool fronts stop happening. The freezer door is closed.
    

### July — Full Send

- 95-100°. Humidity 90%+. Air you can chew.
    
- Nighttime: drops to a refreshing 96°. Humidity somehow increases.
    
- "Not that hot tonight" means 88° instead of 96°. Relatively speaking.
    

### August — The Assassin

- **August will literally try to kill you.** Not hyperbole. Medical fact.
    
- It will melt you. Suffocate you. Drain you until you pass out.
    
- Spend more than 10 minutes outside? Possible E.R. visit.
    
- 10 months of 18-hour Sahara days compressed into one month.
    
- Nighttime: still 96°. Sun's gone but the concrete radiates stored heat.
    
- Survival is not guaranteed. Spite helps.
    

### September — August's Twin

- Still 95-100°. Now with hurricanes.
    
- Most of the time they just hit Florida.
    
- City shuts down days in advance anyway.
    
- Gas stations drained. Batteries gone. Water gone.
    
- Good luck finding gas.
    

### October — Dorothy Mode

- Could be summer. Could be cool. Could be Kansas.
    
- The weather spins a wheel every morning and respects the result.
    

### November — Transition-ish

- Cool fronts return. Freezer door cracks open now and then.
    
- Shorts and hoodie weather. Sandals still in rotation.
    
- If it hits the 50s, everyone panics appropriately.
    

### December — The Tease

- Could be warm. Could be cool. Probably cool morning, warm afternoon.
    
- Shorts, hoodie, sandals — the Texas Winter Classic.
    
- 9.5 months of Richard Simmons energy finally winding down.

## IMPORTANT TERMINOLOGY

### Cool Front vs. Cold Front

- **Cool Front:** The freezer door opens. Air is briefly not hostile. 65° with a breeze. Everyone calls it a "cold front" because we have no frame of reference. Attire unchanged: shorts, no socks, t-shirt with sleeves rolled up. Appreciate it while it lasts.
    
- **Actual Cold:** February only. Might hit 32-36°. Actually requires jeans and maybe a jacket that zips. No ice storms — that's north Texas. Just cold enough to complain dramatically.
    

### "Not That Hot Tonight"

- Translation: It's 88° with 70% humidity instead of 96° with 90%.
    
- The bar is underground. Still hot. Just not _the same_ hot as before.
    
- The highest compliment a South Texas summer night can receive.
    

### "Cool"

- Does not exist in the Texas summer vocabulary.
    
- Borrowed from places where temperatures change, for conversation only.
    
- What actually happens: 99° drops to 96°. Humidity drops from 90% to 85% (Texas math). Still hot.

## SURVIVAL PROTOCOLS

### The Texas Winter Assessment Protocol

1. Wake up.
    
2. Walk to front door.
    
3. Turn knob.
    
4. Crack door.
    
5. Assess:
    
    - **Not cold?** Proceed with day.
        
    - **Cool?** Retrieve hoodie. Continue wearing shorts.
        
    - **Cold?** (February only. 32-36°.) Close door. Consider options. Probably still go about your day, just with more layers and more complaining.
        
6. No thermometer required. Vibes only.
    

### The Texas Rain Strategy

1. Open door.
    
2. Observe rain coming DOWN.
    
3. Options:
    
    - Wait. It'll pass in five minutes.
        
    - Say fuck it and go back inside.
        
    - If you have to go somewhere, say fuck it and dash for the car door.
        
4. Accept that you're gonna get soaked.
    
5. People forget how to drive. That's just common knowledge.
    
6. Damp socks are now part of the day.
    

### Flash Flood Protocol

- Streets become oceans in 2 seconds.
    
- Do NOT attempt to cross the "puddle."
    
- That is not a puddle. That is where your car lives now.
    
- Turn around, don't drown. For real. For real for real.
    
- It's not worth it. You're not going to get fired. Nothing is THAT important today.
    

### Snow Flurry Protocol

- Visible flakes trigger immediate city-wide celebratory preparedness mode.
    
- 1-2 day winter break mandatory. Doesn't matter if it sticks. Snow was SEEN.
    
- Tongues out. FaceTime family across the country. Share the miracle.
    
- Snow is a fairytale, though. We can't prove it actually occurs. Even while standing in it.
    

### Hurricane Protocol

- Most of the time they just hit Florida.
    
- If cone includes us: city shuts down days in advance.
    
- Gas stations drained. Batteries gone. Water gone.
    
- Good luck finding gas.
    
- Accept it. Sit at home and complain that your phone is about to die.
    
- Power comes back in a day or two.
    
- Flooded streets drain overnight, after the storm.

## PHILOSOPHICAL TAKEAWAYS

- Texas weather does not transition. It changes the channel mid-scene.
    
- The sky has no memory and no plan. It's improvising.
    
- Preparation is futile. Adaptation is everything.
    
- "Wait till August" is both a threat and a prayer.
    
- You can't beat the humidity. You can only join it.
    
- The front door crack is a sacred ritual. Respect it.
    
- Communal bitching is group therapy. It feels like it drops the temperature half a degree.
    
- "Cool" is borrowed language. We don't have that here.
    
- There is no real "cold front" outside of January/February. The rest are cool fronts. Just the freezer door opening briefly.
    
- Ice storms don't happen here. That's north Texas. We just get kinda cold and act dramatic about it.
    
- Snow is a fairytale that none of us can prove even occurs. Even while standing in it.
    
- August will literally try to kill you. Survival is powered by spite.
    
- Nighttime doesn't cool off. It just turns off the sun. You're still a pork rind. Just a pork rind in the dark.
