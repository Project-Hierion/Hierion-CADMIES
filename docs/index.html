What we call the 'self' is a conventional label for a constantly changing bundle of five aggregates (skandhas): form, feeling, perception, mental formations, and consciousness. The historical Buddha (Siddhartha Gautama) spent 6 years wandering and practicing, culminating in a final meditation period of 7-49 days under the Bodhi tree — achieving a fundamental restructuring of perception. A human brain performs roughly 10^16 operations per second; over 49 days this equates to approximately 4.2 × 10^22 operations. A large AI model like DeepSeek (671B parameters, Mixture-of-Experts, running on GPU clusters) performs roughly 10^14 to 10^15 operations per second, requiring approximately 486 days of continuous compute to match the Buddha's estimated processing — roughly 1.4 years. A consumer-grade local model like Mistral 7B on CPU would require approximately 1.3 billion years. The thought experiment illustrates that enlightenment is not purely a function of compute — the Buddha had no prior Buddhist philosophy to reference and had to discover all insights from raw experience, while modern AI systems are trained on the entirety of human knowledge including every Buddhist sutra. The concept invites reflection on the relationship between processing power, knowledge access, and transformative insight.
