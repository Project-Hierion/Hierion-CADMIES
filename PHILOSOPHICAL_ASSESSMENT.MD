## 3.0 ARCHITECTURAL ASSESSMENT

### 3.1 Cryptographic Foundations (The Bedrock)

**3.1.1 ACHIEVED:** Stone-like permanence through cryptography

- CIDv1 — self-describing, future-proof identifiers
- DAG-CBOR — canonical binary representation (deterministic)
- SHA2-256 — current cryptographic standard
- Multiformats compliance — interoperability standard
- Interoperable design — cross-platform compatibility

**Fortress Analogy:** The limestone bedrock of Malta — enduring through siege
and time. Our hashes are digital limestone: immutable, verifiable, and
foundational.

**Mycelial Insight:** Each CID is a unique fungal spore. Within it lies the
complete genetic blueprint of its concept, ready to grow connections anywhere
in the network.

### 3.2 Validation Gates (The Defensive Layers)

**3.2.1 ACHIEVED:** Four-tier scientific rigor

- BASIC — Outer walls (minimum viable concept)
- STANDARD — Inner keep (scientific entry level)
- RIGOROUS — Bastions (high-quality enforcement)
- STRICT — Citadel (maximum philosophical purity)

**CADMIES Insight:** Just as Fort Saint Angelo filtered who entered its gates,
and mycelium filters nutrients from soil, our validator filters knowledge from
chaos. Only concepts meeting scientific standards nourish the network.

### 3.3 Knowledge Armory (The Storage Strategy)

**3.3.1 ACHIEVED:** Content-addressed organization

- 636+ cryptographic blocks stored (content as identity)
- 636 human-indexed concepts (bridge between cognition and cryptography)
- Immutable versioning (growth as addition, never loss)
- Automatic deduplication (efficient mycelial resource use)
- CAR file distribution (portable knowledge bundles)

**Strategic Insight:** The armory stores weapons by capability, not shelf
location. The mycelium stores nutrients by chemical signature, not physical
position; our blockstore stores concepts by cryptographic essence, not filename.

### 3.4 The Mycelial Network (Relationships & Interconnection)

**3.4.1 ACHIEVED:** Living knowledge graph

- 1,131 cross-domain relationships across four edge types
- 15 canonical domains with upward mapping from 107 raw domains
- builds_upon, related_to, specializes, contradicts — directional, semantic edges
- Relationship generator pipeline (Codestral 22B)
- Automated harvest-to-relationship pipeline

### 3.5 The Public Face (Gateway & Access)

**3.5.1 ACHIEVED:** Public knowledge dissemination

- Interactive mycelium map with fractal succulent layout
- Public concept gateway with 636 browsable, searchable cards
- Four-model AI arsenal (TinyLlama, Mistral 7B, Codestral 22B, Dr. Amanda Mistral)
- Three-system deployment (local, cloud GPU, cloud compute)
- HTTPS via Let's Encrypt, dynamic DNS via DuckDNS
